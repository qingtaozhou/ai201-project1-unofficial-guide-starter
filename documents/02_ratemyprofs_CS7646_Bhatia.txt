Professor: Bhatia, Ashish
Subject: CS 7646 (Machine Learning for Trading)
Rating: 3.2/5 stars

Review:
Controversial course. People either love it or hate it. Bhatia tries to teach ML applied to stock market prediction, but the course has several issues:

Pros:
- Topic is genuinely interesting and different
- Real-world applicable (quants/hedge funds use these techniques)
- Flexible final project (you can implement your own trading strategy)

Cons:
- Very disorganized. Lectures feel ad-hoc, assignments drop late
- Grading is subjective. Rubric for the project is vague
- You basically teach yourself the material (Bhatia provides minimal guidance)
- Market data quality/availability is an issue
- Some assignments have bugs that TAs don't fix until weeks later

Workload: 15-20 hours/week (depends on your project ambition)
Difficulty: Medium (conceptually, not mathematically rigorous)
Grade: B+ (curved oddly — varies wildly by project)

The hidden challenge: Half your grade is a project where success depends on luck (market conditions) and code quality, not necessarily understanding

Recommendation: Only take if you're genuinely interested in finance ML. If you want solid ML education, take 7641 instead. If you want to build a portfolio project, this is the course for it.
