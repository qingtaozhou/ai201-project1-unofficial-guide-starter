Survey: "Georgia Tech CS Master's Students Rate Their Courses"
Compiled from: r/GATechCS, Discord, Piazza, direct survey responses
Date: May 2026
Responses: 62 Master's students

---

**Question 1: Which course should every MS student take?**

Responses ranked by frequency:
- CS 7641 (ML): 58 mentions (94%)
- CS 6340 (AI): 28 mentions (45%)
- CS 6200 (OS): 22 mentions (35%)
- CS 6505 (Algorithms): 19 mentions (31%, but divisive)

Key quote: "7641 is the only course that pays dividends after graduation. Do it." - Industry intern

**Question 2: What's the hardest course at Tech (Master's level)?**

1. CS 6505 (Algorithms): 31 mentions
2. CS 7641 (ML): 19 mentions (people found it hard, but fair)
3. CS 7646 (Trading): 8 mentions (hard but for different reasons — disorganization)
4. CS 6476 (Vision): 4 mentions

Key quote: "6505 is designed to make you question your life choices. But if you pass, you've proved you can think." - PhD-track student

**Question 3: How should students choose their course load?**

Most common strategy:
- Take 2 hard + 1 medium course per semester (18 credits)
- Examples: (7641 + 6505 + 6300) or (7641 + 6340 + 6200)
- Avoid: 3 hard courses (7641 + 6505 + 7646)

Time estimates they reported:
- Hard courses: 14-18 hrs/week
- Medium courses: 10-13 hrs/week
- Light courses: 7-10 hrs/week

**Question 4: How should industry-track vs PhD-track students choose differently?**

Industry track (FAANG, quant, ML engineering):
1. CS 7641 (ML) — non-negotiable
2. CS 6340 (AI) — good breadth
3. CS 6200 (OS) — systems understanding
4. Skip: CS 6505 (Algorithms) unless going to PhD later

PhD track (Academia, research):
1. CS 6505 (Algorithms) — foundational proof techniques
2. CS 7641 (ML) — research is data-driven
3. CS 6340 (AI) — foundational AI theory
4. Skip: CS 7646 (Trading) unless finance research interest

**Question 5: What's the biggest mistake students make?**

Top 3:
1. Taking too many hard courses at once (38 mentions)
2. Underestimating 7641 workload (25 mentions)
3. Comparing themselves to PhD students who have advantages (18 mentions)

Key quotes:
- "I took 7641 + 6505 at the same time. I aged 10 years in 4 months." - Industry student
- "The homework for 7641 says 10 hours but takes 20 if you actually understand it." - Master's student
- "Don't compare yourself to PhD students. They've had a semester to settle in. You're new." - Grad advisor

**Question 6: Professor quality matters — who should you seek out?**

Best rated professors:
- Isbell (CS 7641): 4.8/5 (comprehensive, fair, cares)
- Doroudi (CS 6340): 4.7/5 (great teacher, well-organized)
- Goldberg (CS 6505): 4.5/5 (intimidating but rewarding, fair grading)

Problematic:
- Bhatia (CS 7646): 3.2/5 (disorganized, great topic, rough execution)
