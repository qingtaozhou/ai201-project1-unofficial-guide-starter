Blog Post: "CS 7641 vs CS 6340: Which ML/AI course should you actually take?"
Author: Georgia Tech CS Master's Graduate (2025)
Date: May 2026

Comparison:

I took both 7641 and 6340 as a Master's student. Here's what they are and which one you should prioritize.

**CS 7641 — Machine Learning (Supervised/Unsupervised/RL)**

What it covers:
- Supervised learning (regression, classification)
- Unsupervised learning (clustering, dimensionality reduction)
- Reinforcement learning (Q-learning, policy gradients)
- Neural networks and deep learning intro
- Ensemble methods, Bayesian approaches

Vibe: Rigorous, mathematical, extensive. Feels like a university ML course should.

Workload: Heavy (14-16 hrs/week)

Why it matters:
- If you want to be an ML engineer, this is the foundation course
- Every ML recruiter expects it on your resume
- The homework is applied (build decision trees, implement neural nets)
- You actually understand WHY algorithms work

Math level: High (linear algebra, calculus, probability)

Professor: Isbell teaches it well, fair exams, generous curve

---

**CS 6340 — Artificial Intelligence (Search, Planning, Game Playing)**

What it covers:
- Search algorithms (BFS, DFS, A*, minimax)
- Constraint satisfaction problems (backtracking, heuristics)
- Game playing (adversarial search)
- Knowledge representation (logic, Bayesian networks)
- Planning and uncertainty

Vibe: Algorithmic, practical problem-solving. More "implement cool things" than "understand deep theory."

Workload: Medium (11-13 hrs/week)

Why it matters:
- Broader CS perspective (not just data science)
- Good for game AI, robotics, planning problems
- Algorithm design skills are timeless
- Professor Doroudi is excellent

Math level: Medium (basic probability, logic)

Professor: Doroudi is phenomenal (clear, helpful, organized)

---

**Head-to-Head Comparison:**

| Aspect | 7641 ML | 6340 AI |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Difficulty | Hard | Medium |
| Math burden | Heavy (linalg, calc, prob) | Light (basic prob, logic) |
| Implementation | Build ML from scratch | Implement algorithms |
| Time commitment | 15+ hrs/week | 11 hrs/week |
| Job relevance | Critical for ML roles | Good for breadth |
| Research relevance | Very high | Moderate |
| Fun factor | Intellectually satisfying | Algorithm problem-solving |
| Curve | Generous | Depends on class performance |
| Professor quality | Excellent | Excellent |

---

**Decision Tree:**

Do you want to work in ML?
→ YES: Take 7641. Non-negotiable. Take 6340 as second course.
→ NO, but interested in AI/algorithms: Take 6340, skip 7641.
→ UNSURE: Take 7641 first. If you enjoy it, continue. If you hate it, 6340 is good pivot.

Are you PhD-track?
→ YES: Take both. Start with 6505 (algorithms) + 7641. Defer 6340 to second semester.
→ NO (industry-track): 7641 is essential, 6340 is optional but recommended.

---

**My Personal Take:**

I did 7641 first semester (suffered but learned deeply).
I did 6340 second semester (enjoyed it more, easier pace).

In retrospect, this order made sense. 7641 is the harder, more essential course. I was fresh and could handle it. 6340 was a nice relief that reinforced broader CS thinking.

If I were advising someone:
- Do 7641 when you're fresh (ideally semester 1)
- Do 6340 as a complementary course (semester 1 or 2, doesn't matter)
- Skip CS 6505 unless PhD-track or masochistic
