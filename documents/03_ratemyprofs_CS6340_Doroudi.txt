Professor: Doroudi, Samira
Subject: CS 6340 (Artificial Intelligence)
Rating: 4.7/5 stars

Review:
Doroudi is phenomenal. AI is a broad course covering search, game playing, constraint satisfaction, knowledge representation, and planning. She teaches it beautifully — complex concepts become intuitive.

The course structure:
- Homework: Implement AI algorithms (minimax, A* search, CSP solvers). Very hands-on.
- Exams: Require deep understanding of why algorithms work, not just memorization
- Project: Open-ended AI problem of your choice (chatbots, game AIs, pathfinding, etc.)

Why it's excellent:
- Doroudi clearly explains the intuition before diving into implementation
- Office hours are gold — she'll help you think through problems
- The homework solutions are released after deadlines, helpful for learning
- Curve is generous if the class struggles

Workload: 10-12 hours/week (well-paced)
Difficulty: Medium (math is minimal, implementation is primary challenge)
Grade: A (curved to ~80% median)

Prerequisites: Comfortable with programming, basic math

Similar to: CS 7641 in terms of rigor but less math, more algorithm design

One caveat: If your background is only undergrad CS, you might struggle with the math framework early on. But Doroudi teaches it clearly so it's fine.
