Title: "Worth the money? Honest take after finishing my degree"

Post:

I just finished my CS Master's at Georgia Tech. Total cost: ~$35k over 2 years (part-time, taking 2 courses/semester). Here's my honest assessment.

**Career outcome:**
Got offered 3 jobs after graduation, all in ML/AI. Highest offer: $180k TC (San Francisco). Current: $165k TC (remote, Atlanta-based company). Previously: $120k TC before grad school.

**Was it worth it?**
Yes, but with caveats.

**What mattered:**
1. CS 7641 on transcript (recruiters specifically asked about it)
2. Projects I built for classes (portfolio matters more than GPA)
3. Networking with classmates (2 of my job connections came from study groups)
4. Time to build side projects while studying

**What didn't matter:**
- GPA (mine was 3.8 but nobody asked for it)
- Taking obscure electives (CS 7646 seemed cool but wasn't valuable)
- Rushing to finish (taking it part-time gave me breathing room)

**Red flags I saw (students who struggled):**
- Taking too many courses too fast (8 courses in 1 year)
- Prioritizing GPA over actual learning (students who got A's but couldn't code)
- Skipping the hard courses (7641, 6505) for easier electives
- Not building portfolio projects (grades alone don't get jobs)

**Best strategy I saw:**
- Full-time MS: 3 semesters, 2 courses per semester, 1 internship
- Part-time MS: 4-5 semesters, 2 courses per semester, keep your job
- Prioritize: 7641 (ML), then your domain of interest, then electives
- Build 1-2 portfolio projects alongside courses
- Network with TAs and professors (they have connections)

**Would I do it again?**
Yes. The salary increase alone paid for itself. Plus I understand ML deeply now (not just API-driven coding).

**Why some people regret it:**
- Took non-rigorous electives instead of hard ones
- Didn't job search properly (good resume still matters)
- Isolated themselves (didn't network with cohort)
- Tried to do too much too fast

**The reality check:**
The degree opens doors, but YOU have to walk through them. There are graduates who job-searched for 6 months. There are graduates who had 5 offers. Difference was interview prep and being selective about courses.

**My coursework (in order):**
Semester 1: 7641 (ML), 6340 (AI) — foundation courses
Semester 2: 6200 (OS), 6300 (SWE) — systems understanding
Semester 3: 7646 (Trading), 6001 (Bio) — electives (honestly didn't matter)
Internship: Summer, 3 months in ML research

This gave me: ML foundation + systems knowledge + research experience + portfolio projects.

**TL;DR:**
- Get your 7641 + domain-specific courses
- Build projects
- Network
- Don't get caught up in GPA
- The degree is an accelerant, not a guarantee
