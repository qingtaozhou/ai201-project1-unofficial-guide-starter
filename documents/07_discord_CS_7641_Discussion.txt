User A (2nd semester):
Not a quick answer but: Yes, take 7641. I was also considering skipping it and just doing 6340. But here's why 7641 matters:

**7641 is hard but it's the foundation.** 6340 is AI algorithms (search, planning, CSP). 7641 is learning from data (the thing everyone actually does). If you want an ML job, employers basically expect you to have 7641-level knowledge.

**The pain is worth it:** I spent 20 hours on homework for 7641. I spent 12 hours on 6340 homework. But the 7641 homework actually taught me how to think about ML. The 6340 homework was "implement this algorithm."

**6340 is great BUT:** It's more CS-theory adjacent. If your goal is ML (which I assume it is if you're asking), 6340 is a nice supplement, not a replacement.

User B (1st semester):
Okay but I'm scared I'll fail. What if I'm not smart enough?

User A:
You won't fail. Isbell curves. The class is hard but ~60% pass with B or better. If you don't fail all exams, the homework grades bring you up.

Also: "not smart enough" is not the barrier. The barrier is time. If you give 7641 15 hours a week, you'll pass. If you give it 5 hours, you'll struggle.

User C (Master's advisor):
Co-signing this. We tell students: if you have time for one course, make it 7641. Everything else is supporting.

The grad recruiters at tech companies (FAANG, quant firms) want to see 7641 on your transcript. They don't care about 6340.

User B:
Alright, I'm convinced. But how do I balance it with other courses?

User A:
Take 7641 + 1 medium course. Don't stack it with 6505 (too hard) or 7646 (project hell).

Good pairings:
- 7641 + 6340 (both math-heavy, different flavors)
- 7641 + 6200 (7641 is theory, 6200 is systems, they don't overlap)
- 7641 + 6300 (6300 is lighter, gives you breathing room)

Bad pairings:
- 7641 + 6505 (suicide mission)
- 7641 + 7646 + anything (all time-hungry)

User D (Part-time student):
How do I do 7641 part-time? I work full-time.

User A:
Honestly? You don't, easily. 7641 is designed for full-time students. The exams are timed and require deep understanding, not quick solutions.

But some people do it:
- Start homework first day it drops
- Go to office hours even if just to listen
- Use fall semester (lighter load) to do it
- Or do it solo-course semester

I wouldn't recommend it. Better to add an extra semester than to crush 7641 while working full-time.
